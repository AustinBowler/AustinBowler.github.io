Contrast

In web design

What is contrast?

According to dictionary.com, contrast is defined as an object that exhibits “unlikeness on comparison with something else.”

In other words… something that is different or out of place

There are four different kinds of contrast in design

Color

Size

Shape

Type

Contrast in Color

Put simply, contrast in color is determined by how different two colors are. The greater the difference, the higher the contrast.

For example, this picture displays colors that have high contrast, but colors like black and grey have a low contrast

Contrast in Color

However, colors with low contrast can still be useful, as they create depth in objects

The contrasting blue and orange in this picture direct the attention of the eye, but the red lines and shading behind the car add depth

Contrast in Color

In terms of web design, colors are spread across different elements of each page, making each element significantly different.

This web page made their menu items darker, and articles lighter. This effectively separates different kinds of information.

Contrast in Color

Contrast in color can also refer to color between textual elements

This article’s title has been changed in color as well as the subtitle and body text to attract the eye to each section in order. This also separates each element.

Contrast in Size

Contrast in size is the difference in size between elements

For example, human eyes are drawn to the larger images, like in this picture where one notices the word “Big” first.

Contrast in Size

Or in this web page where your eyes are attracted to the quote at the top, and then to the second largest items: the pictures.

Contrast in Shape

Contrast in shape is meant to rip attention away from the rest of the format and focus it on one thing, like the lamp in this web page.

Contrast in Type

Contrast in type simply means the difference used between different fonts and typefaces. A completely uniform page is boring, so small variations in boldness, size, or even font are attractive to the mind and eye.

This web page shows a great example by varying the size and boldness of title and subtitle elements.

Why is contrast a good thing?

Contrast separates elements and makes them unique and easy to find. Without it, each webpage and graphic would look like an amorphous blob; your mind hopelessly searching to find exactly what you need.
