A Special Thanks

Legs

Although I would love to buy rubber legs, or "Bermuda shorts", as they were called backstage. However, they are quite expensive as offered by B9 Robot Builders Club Vendors. Rather, I believe I can use foam insulation tubes (similar to pool-noodles). Then I can buy some liquid rubber and pour paint in it untill it is the right shade of grey, using a paintbrush or something to apply to the tubes. As for the waistplate and dounut (after the torso but before the legs), I will use the pool noodle and cardboard technique described below.

Knees

The knees start at the point in the legs where the two metal plates extrude. The knee hinges are flat, and attatched to the side, if you look close you can see it. The plates are thick, and I do say that I do NOT want to cut that thick of metal, so I will stack cardboard and cover it with a paper mache or clay, smooth, prepare, and paint.
